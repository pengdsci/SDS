Objective and Rationale

Objective
To create a BS degree in Statistics and Data Science (SDS) to increase the program enrollment and improve retention.

Rationale
1. The enrollment in statistics concentration has decreased significantly in recent semesters. The statistics minor enrollment is also declining.

F2021 S2022 F2022 S2023 F2023 S2024
Stat Concentration 41 45 46 44 37 26
Stat Minor 73 54 57 38 41 45

On the other hand, the job growth in the field stays strong. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, data scientist and statistician positions in the job market continue to be among the fastest-growing jobs. The projected increase in job openings in data science and statistics from 2022 to 2032 is 35% and 30%, respectively (see the official release of occupational outlook at the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics.).

2. The field of statistics and data science is constantly evolving with new methods and technologies. It is of vital importance to stay current with the latest advancements through continuous curricular development.

3. Fast-emerging technologies and innovative methodologies are causing workplace desired skills to evolve continuously. To equip students with desired skills, it is necessary to have a discipline-specific and job-market-relevant curriculum that is accessible to more students who want to pursue a career in the field with a mathematics core comparable to that of some of the successful Statistics BS programs at some of the major institutions such as Notre Dame, George Mason, Brigham Young, etc.

List of Statistics and Data Science Courses

All courses in the following list carry 3 credits except for STA496, STA498, and STA499.

STA 200. Statistics II (tentative topics available) +

Continuation of MAT 121/MAT 125. Topics include inference about the means, standard deviations and proportions, goodness of fit, analysis of variance, regression analysis, correlation, and nonparametric tests. In addition, students will learn to use SPSS, a statistical analysis software that is available for free through the Ram Cloud. Finally, students will hone their technical writing skills by summarizing their statistical analyses with written reports.
Pre / Co requisites: STA 200 requires a prerequisite of a grade of C- or better in MAT 121, MAT 125 or ECO 251.
Distance education offering may be available.
Typically offered in Fall & Spring.

Tentative Topics

STA 201. Statistical Methods for Political Polling +

The purpose of this course is to introduce students to the statistical methodology used in the analysis of data from a political survey. Topics will include sampling techniques, hypothesis testing, sample size calculation, categorical data analysis, simple linear regression, and ANOVA. There will be a field trip to the Center for Opinion Research at Franklin and Marshall College at the beginning of the semester.
Pre / Co requisites: STA 201 requires a prerequisite of MAT 121 or PSC 200.
Typically offered in Fall.

STA 202. Sports Analytics +

In this course we will apply concepts such as regression, classification, clustering, decision trees and others to evaluate players and teams from baseball, basketball, football, hockey and soccer. We will also introduce the statistical programming language R in order to analyze recent (and large!) data sets.
Pre / Co requisites: STA 202 requires a prerequisite of a C- or higher in MAT 121, MAT 125, or ECO 251.
Typically offered in Fall.

STA 203. Probability and Statistics in Gaming +

In this class students will learn the important role that probability and statistics play in the enjoyment and development of games ranging from blackjack and the lottery to Battleship and World of Warcraft. Statistical topics include descriptive statistics, probability, discrete random variables, and multivariate linear modeling. Throughout the course students will use statistical tools to not only develop optimal strategies while gaming but also to analyze the current trends in contemporary gaming, determine which factors correlate with a game's popularity, and how to develop algorithms for computer opponents.
Pre / Co requisites: STA 203 requires a prerequisite of MAT 121 or MAT 125.
Typically offered in Spring.

STA 428. Statistical Methods for Data Science (tentative topics unavailable, will be compiled once I receive the topics from STA419) +

This course introduces statistical methods used in data science and data science algorithms rooted in statistics to reflect the symbiotic relationship between the two subjects. Students will gain hands-on experience in integrating tools in both subjects to solve real-world problems. Topics include probabilistic and statistical models such as naive Bayes, algorithmic-based regression models, and machine learning tools such as tree-based algorithms, latent regression for dimension reduction, support vector machines, and neural networks.
